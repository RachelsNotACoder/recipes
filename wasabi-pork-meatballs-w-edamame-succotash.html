Wasabi Pork Meatballs w Edamame “Succotash”

Prep / Total 15m / 1h 5m
Tags
Multi Asian Pork
Servings
6

Wasabi and ginger-spiced pork meatballs (with a sneaky bit of pureed squash for moisture) roast alongside a quick stir-fried corn and edamame succotash.

Ingredients

Preparation

  1. 01

    Mix all the meatball ingredients until just combined and refrigerate mixture for about 30 minutes. Preheat oven to 450’ while you chop and measure the vegetables for the succotash.

  2. 02

    Divide meatball mixture into 30 portions and shape into meatballs. Place the meatballs on the sheet pan in a single layer. Roast for 20 minutes. You may want to take the sheet pan out and rotate halfway through to promote even roasting if your oven has hot spots.

  3. 03

    About 10 minutes before meatballs are finished baking, heat a large skillet on high. Add the oil and all the veggies and stir fry until vegetables are crisp-tender, about 5 minutes. Add salt and soy sauce then remove to serving bowl. Remove meatballs from oven and serve.

Tips and Notes

  • Yes, pumpkin. In the meatballs. I've found that a little pureed squash added to meatballs, meatloaves, and even burgers helps keep the meat moist and tender. It does not impart a squashy or pumpkiny flavor to the meat at all. Canned pumpkin is handy for this. In the winter I've generally got an open can around all the time for applications like this, but also because I like to stir a couple of tablespoons of it into my cocoa, my coffee, my yogurt, my oatmeal, etc. If you have leftover roasted whole squash of any kind, a little bit of it mashed works too.
  • I tend to keep a paste on hand in my freezer that is made of equal parts ginger root and garlic (and just use a couple of tablespoons or so of it in the meatballs and the succotash). That paste is super-useful in Japanese, Chinese, Thai, and Indian-inspired recipes.

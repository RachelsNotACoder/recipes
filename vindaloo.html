Pork Meatball Vindaloo

Prep / Total 30m / 1h 5m
Tags
Saucepan Indian Pork Ground Meat
Servings
4

A classic Goan vindaloo base — built from scratch with whole toasted spices and a caramelized onion paste — wrapped around tender pork meatballs and finished with sweet peppers and grape tomatoes.

Ingredients

Preparation

  1. 01

    Using a mortar and pestle or spice grinder, grind the cumin seeds, peppercorns, coriander seeds, and black mustard seeds until fine. Transfer to a small bowl and stir in the ground cardamom, ground fenugreek, cinnamon, turmeric, chile powder, salt, brown sugar, and vinegar. Set aside.

  2. 02

    Heat vegetable oil in a sauté pan over medium heat until shimmering. Add onions and cook, stirring often, until browned and crisp around the edges, about 12 minutes. Add ginger and garlic and cook 2 minutes more. Transfer to a blender and purée until smooth, adding water a tablespoon at a time until a paste forms. Stir into the spice mixture.

  3. 03

    Combine bread cubes and milk in a large bowl and let stand for 10 minutes. Drain, squeezing the bread dry; discard the milk. Mix the soaked bread thoroughly with the pork, egg, garlic, and lemon zest. Season with salt. Using wet hands, form the mixture into 10 golf-ball-sized balls.

  4. 04

    Warm about 4 tablespoons of the spice base in a large pot over medium-low heat. Add the canned tomatoes with their juices and stir to combine — taste and add more vindaloo paste and salt if needed. Gently nestle the meatballs into the sauce and simmer, basting periodically, for 10 minutes.

  5. 05

    Gently stir in the red and yellow bell peppers, jalapeño, and grape tomatoes. Continue cooking until the vegetables are just tender and the meatballs are cooked through, about 5 minutes more.

  6. 06

    Ladle into bowls and finish with a scattering of fresh cilantro. Flatbread or basmati rice alongside if you want something to soak up the sauce.

Tips and Notes

  • The spice base makes more than you need — leftovers keep refrigerated for a week and work beautifully as a marinade for chicken or lamb.
  • Ground cardamom and fenugreek are used here in place of the whole seeds: add them directly to the spice bowl without toasting.
  • The paste's heat level is moderate; add a second jalapeño or leave the seeds in if you want more bite.

Olive Chicken with Chickpeas

Prep / Total 15m / 30m
Tags
Skillet S Europe Chicken
Servings
6

A colorful, quick-cooking skillet of chicken and mushrooms with peppers, chickpeas, spinach, and a mix of olives in a smoked paprika and herbes de provence base.

Ingredients

Preparation

  1. 01

    Combine the chickpeas with the spinach, olives, and tomato paste and set aside.

  2. 02

    Heat a large skillet or wok on high heat. Add 1 tbsp of the oil with the chicken and mushrooms and cook, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is browned, about 5 minutes.

  3. 03

    Reduce the heat to medium and add the rest of the oil with the onion and bell pepper. Cook, stirring, for about 5 minutes, until the onions have softened.

  4. 04

    Add the garlic and spices and cook, stirring, for about 1 minute. Add the chickpea, olive and spinach mixture to the skillet and cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally to combine.

Tips and Notes

  • This is a colorful and flavorful dish that comes together quite quickly. It's pretty flexible about vegetables: you could add tomatoes, zucchini, broccoli, artichoke hearts, etc.
  • You could use white beans (cannellini, navy, white kidney, etc.) instead of chickpeas if you like, especially if you want a more Italian feel for the dish. I really like the hearty and chewy texture of the chickpeas here though.
  • It would be delicious to use pesto instead of tomato paste here, too; but in that case, add the pesto right before serving and don't cook it with the dish (since that would mess up the cheesy texture of pesto). Other flavors that would work well would be capers and/or an anchovy or two, to make it almost a chickpea putanesca sort of dish.
  • If you prefer to use fresh spinach, that would absolutely work and be beautiful -- but the spinach will start out with a lot of volume in your pan to manage. If you go that route, add the spinach on its own at the very end by just piling it on top of everything in the skillet, then cover with a heavy lid for a minute or two to wilt it.

Southwest Chicken Soup

Prep / Total 15m / 1h 30m
Tags
Stockpot SW Chicken
Servings
6

A hearty, forgiving stovetop soup of chicken, beans, zucchini, and corn in a cumin-chili tomato broth, finished with a handful of spinach.

Ingredients

Preparation

  1. 01

    Heat a large lidded stockpot on medium high heat and add the oil and the chicken. Cook for 5-10 minutes, until some brownness develops on the chicken and in the bottom of your pan.

  2. 02

    Add the onions and peppers and cook, stirring frequently, until the veggies are softened and their liquid has started to break down the golden-brown fond at the bottom of the pot from browning the meat, about 10 minutes.

  3. 03

    Add spices and cook for another 1 minute, until fragrant.

  4. 04

    Reduce heat to low and add the corn, zucchini, tomatoes, salsa, beans, and the stock. Cover and cook for 1 to 2 hours (it's forgiving!). Add the spinach about 5 minutes before taking the soup off the heat -- just long enough to thaw it out.

Tips and Notes

  • This is another soup so lean and light that I prefer to serve it with a little bit of something fatty: diced avocado or shredded cheddar cheese on top, or even a spoonful of sour cream.
  • You can lighten this up further just by adding more vegetables: an extra bell pepper or more zucchini.
  • A fantastic topping for this soup is a tablespoon (per serving) of popped quinoa. Just preheat a dry skillet over medium high heat and then toss in some dry uncooked quinoa. Shake the skillet around a bit so it doesn't scorch, and the quinoa will pop like popcorn (but less ballistically, so you don't need a lid) in about one minute or less. It adds a very pleasing crunch along with a nice toasted, nutty flavor.
  • As with many soups, the sodium count here is pretty daunting. The nutritional information below was calculated using standard sodium products though, and you can reduce the sodium considerably by choosing low or no salt added beans and broth, and reading labels on salsa (if you don't make your own). Salsa in particular varies quite a bit in sodium content depending on brand (I find that as with many products, the Safeway house brand is very reasonable in sodium content). If you can find low salt crushed tomatoes so much the better, but I have rarely seen those.

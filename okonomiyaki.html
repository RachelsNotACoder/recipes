Shrimp & Bacon Okonomiyaki

Adapted from Serious Eats / J. Kenji López-Alt — halved for one generous serving

Serves 1
Active time ~15 min
Cook time ~20 min
Pan 10" nonstick

Ingredients

  • 2 packed cups cabbage, finely shredded
  • 1 large egg
  • ¼ cup cold water
  • ⅜ cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 oz bacon, chopped
  • 4 oz shrimp, peeled, deveined, roughly chopped
  • ¼ red onion, diced
  • to serve mayo (optional)

Method

1
Make the batter
Combine cabbage, egg, water, and flour in a large bowl. Beat heavily with a fork until a thick, bubbly batter forms. Set aside.
2
Cook bacon
Add chopped bacon to a 10-inch nonstick skillet over medium heat. Cook until nearly crisp, about 6–7 minutes.
⏱ ~7 min
3
Add shrimp and onion
Add shrimp and red onion to the pan. Cook, stirring occasionally, until shrimp is just pink and onion has softened slightly, about 2–3 minutes.
⏱ ~3 min
4
Add batter and cook first side
Pour batter over the mix-ins and spread into an even layer with a fork. Cover and cook, shaking the pan occasionally, until the bottom is crisp and well browned, about 10 minutes. Lower heat if it starts to burn.
⏱ ~10 min
5
Flip and finish
Drain any excess fat. Flip with a wide spatula — run it around the edges first to loosen. Return to heat, cover, and cook until the second side is browned and the center is set but still a little custardy, about 8 minutes.
⏱ ~8 min
6
Serve
Transfer to a plate and serve immediately. Top with mayo if you like.
Note: The flip is the trickiest part. Run a flexible spatula around the edges to loosen before going for it. A wide spatula works fine here — no need for the lid method the original recipe calls for.

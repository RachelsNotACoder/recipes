Lemongrass and Lime Fish Soup

Prep / Total 15m / 35m
Tags
Stockpot Asian White Fish
Servings
6

A bright, aromatic stovetop soup where lemongrass, lime, and chili perfume a quick broth for flaky white fish, mushrooms, and snow peas.

Ingredients

Preparation

  1. 01

    Heat a large lidded stockpot on medium heat and add half the oil with the mushrooms and cook for a couple of minutes to begin to soften.

  2. 02

    Add the remaining oil to the pan with the green onions, garlic, lemongrass, and chili and continue to cook, stirring, for about 1 minute to soften.

  3. 03

    Add the wine and increase the heat to high. Stir, scraping the bottom of the pan, until the wine begins to bubble, then add the vegetable stock with the fish sauce and lime juice. Continue stirring until the liquid reaches a simmer and then add the fish and the snow peas. If the soup looks too “crowded” feel free to add more water here. Bring to a simmer again, then reduce the heat to allow to continue to simmer gently for about 5 minutes to cook the fish through.

Tips and Notes

  • This stovetop soup makes for a very quick light meal. Feel free to add more vegetables like shredded carrot to bulk it out, or even have it with rice noodles. I've had this with some leftover spaghetti squash and found that a surprisingly nice combination!

Mediterranean Farro Bowl with Salmon

Prep / Total 15m / 55m
Tags
Stockpot S Europe Salmon
Servings
6

Chewy farro simmers with mushrooms, sun-dried tomatoes, and fennel seed, then gets topped with flaked roasted salmon, pine nuts, and olives.

Ingredients

Preparation

  1. 01

    Preheat your oven to 400’ and prepare a sheetpan with foil for easy cleanup of the salmon.

  2. 02

    Heat a large saucepan over medium heat with the olive oil, and cook the onion, pepper, and mushrooms until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the sundried tomatoes, fennel seed, oregano and garlic and cook for 1 minute more, until fragrant.

  3. 03

    Add the farro to the pan and stir until the grains are coated with the oil and moisture from the vegetables, and cook for one minute more.

  4. 04

    Add the liquid to the pan, raise heat to high, and bring to a boil. Lower the heat to a bare simmer, cover, and cook until farro has absorbed the moisture. Time will vary depending on how processed your farro is, but 20-30 minutes is what I would expect. Just refer to the package directions on your farro.

  5. 05

    5-10 minutes before you expect the farro to be done cooking, put the salmon in the oven and cook lightly. 5 minutes should be sufficient -- a little underdone is good here, since it will keep cooking once you remove it from the oven.

  6. 06

    Remove the salmon from the oven, flake the flesh off the skin, and stir into the farro. Serve topped with pine nuts and olives, if desired.

Tips and Notes

  • You can always substitute barley for farro in a pinch, it behaves pretty similarly in the pot. Could you use quinoa instead of farro? Sure! Quinoa cooks much more quickly than farro, so adjust accordingly. I like the comparative chewiness of farro here because it's a great contrast to the tenderness of the salmon.
  • Grain bowls are also a great way to use up vegetable odds and ends in your kitchen. Other veggies that would go great here would be fennel bulb or broccoli. You could stir in some baby spinach right before serving (just enough to wilt it). Or you could chop kale and add it at the same time as the farro so it has time to cook more thoroughly. Or add frozen spinach, etc. etc. etc.
  • There are really any number of approaches you can take to the salmon -- roasting it for a few minutes seems pretty low-fuss to me. But if you prefer, you can skin it, dice into small chunks, and stir into the farro about 10 minutes before serving and let it poach with the farro. You can also use leftover salmon -- as usual, grain bowls are a great way to use up leftovers!
  • If you can get your hands on fresh sardines that have already been cleaned off the bones, those would make an excellent alternative to the salmon here.

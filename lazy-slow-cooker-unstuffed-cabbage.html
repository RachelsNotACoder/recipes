Lazy Slow Cooker “Unstuffed” Cabbage

Prep / Total 15m / 6h 30m
Tags
Crockpot US Beef
Servings
6

All the flavor of stuffed cabbage rolls without the rolling — ground beef, bulgur, and cabbage layered in the slow cooker with a lightly Middle Eastern-spiced tomato broth.

Ingredients

Preparation

  1. 01

    Place the onion in the bottom of the slow cooker, then put the ground beef on top. Sprinkle the bulgur over the beef.

  2. 02

    Mix the tomatoes, spices, and wine, the pour over the beef and bulgur.

  3. 03

    Finally, put the cabbage on top. It will cook more evenly if you make sure you break the leaves apart if you used a tightly closed headed cabbage.

  4. 04

    Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 6-7 hours. I recommend uncovering and giving everything a good stir after the first 4 hours to break up the beef.

Tips and Notes

  • I really love cabbage rolls, but honestly, all that effort to soften the cabbage leaves up and then roll everything up -- who needs it? Here’s a lazy deconstructed “unstuffed” cabbage roll stew in the slow cooker. This version has a slight middle-eastern flavor instead of the common eastern European versions you often see.
  • Higher-fat ground beef is excellent for grilled food like hamburgers or baked meatballs so all that fat “bastes” the meat and keeps it moist but mostly drains from the final product. In stews and soups, a leaner mix is good idea since moisture is not a problem and you don’t want the final result to be greasy.
  • As usual, there’s a method to my uptight madness with the layering order. Onions in the bottom helps make sure the bottom has enough moisture to prevent sticking. Bulgur above (rather than below) the beef will make sure the bulgur has access to the moisture from the tomatoes. Cabbage on top because it’s moist enough on its own that you don’t need to worry about whether it’s submerged in the liquid the whole time.

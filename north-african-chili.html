North African "Chili"

Prep / Total 15m / 50m
Tags
Stockpot African Beef Lamb
Servings
6

A no-fuss, spice-forward riff on a Tunisian meatball recipe — ground meat simmered straight into a cumin-cinnamon tomato and chickpea sauce with zucchini.

Ingredients

Preparation

  1. 01

    Heat the olive oil in the bottom of a dutch oven or soup pot over medium heat and add the onions. Cook, stirring occasionally, until onions are softened, about 2-4 minutes.

  2. 02

    Add the garlic and spices and cook, stirring, for about 30 seconds, until spices are fragrant.

  3. 03

    Add the ground meat and brown thoroughly, about 3-5 minutes. If you used very fatty meat, you may need or want to pour off any rendered fat. When I use lean beef or even lamb, I don't find it necessary, but I don't use shoulder lamb or high fat beef.

  4. 04

    Add the diced tomatoes and chickpeas, cover pot, and lower heat to a simmer. Cook for 30 minutes.

  5. 05

    Uncover the pot and add the zucchini. Raise the heat slightly and cook for another 10 minutes or so to tighten the chili and cook the zucchini through.

Tips and Notes

  • This is actually a no-fuss version of an old Tunisian meatball recipe I've loved for years. One day I came home and was craving the dish, but didn't want to mess with assembling and cooking meatballs separately from making the spicy tomato sauce. "Will it chili?" I wondered. It does!
  • There are a couple of other options for getting some more vegetables along with this dish. Instead of adding the zucchini at the end, you can always stir in some greens. Simply roasted cauliflower as a side is also very nice (cut up one head of cauliflower, toss flowerets in olive oil, dust with cumin and maybe a pinch of ginger, and roast on a sheet pan at 400 degrees for 30 minutes).

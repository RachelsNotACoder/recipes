Balsamic Braised Chicken w Zucchini, Eggplant and Tomato

Prep / Total 20m / 1h
Tags
Skillet US Chicken
Servings
6

Chicken thighs braise quickly in a balsamic sauce with mushrooms, eggplant, and zucchini for a saucy, flavorful one-pan supper.

Ingredients

Preparation

  1. 01

    Preheat a large covered skillet or a dutch oven on medium high, then add the oil and chicken thighs. Brown the chicken on all sides, about 5 minutes total, then remove from the pan.

  2. 02

    Reduce the heat to medium and add the onions and peppers. Cook, stirring frequently, about 5 minutes more.

  3. 03

    Add the garlic and spices, stir to combine and cook about 1 minute.

  4. 04

    Add the mushrooms and eggplant cubes, along with the diced tomatoes, broth, and balsamic vinegar. Bring to a simmer then cover for 20 minutes.

  5. 05

    After 20 minutes, add the zucchini to the pot, re-cover, then cook for 10 minutes more.

  6. 06

    Finally, return the chicken pieces to the pot and continue cooking for 10-15 minutes until the zucchini reaches your desired level of tenderness. I like mine to still have a little bite! The chicken should be cooked through in 5-7 minutes.

  7. 07

    Remove the lid. If the liquid is still too “saucy” for you, increase the heat to medium high and cook for up to 5 more minutes to reduce the sauce. Otherwise, serve immediately.

Tips and Notes

  • Chicken thighs braise quickly in a balsamic sauce with summer vegetables for a quick and flavorful supper.
  • Personally, I generally prefer boneless meat: it leaves more room in the pan for vegetables, and the final dish is more convenient for portioning (I can have big portions or small portions without regard to how many pieces of chicken are in there). But if you prefer to work with bone in chicken thighs, they will work well here too. If you use bone-in thighs, I would say add them back to the pot at the same time as the zucchini to give them more time to cook.

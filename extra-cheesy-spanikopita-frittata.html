Extra Cheesy "Spanikopita" Frittata

Prep / Total 10m / 25m
Tags
Skillet Greek Eggs
Servings
4

All the spinach-and-feta flavor of spanikopita without the fussy phyllo — this frittata layers wilted spinach, chickpeas, feta, and cheddar into a quick stovetop-to-broiler egg bake.

Ingredients

Preparation

  1. 01

    Crack the eggs into a large bowl with oregano, salt and pepper, mix them with a fork or a whisk.

  2. 02

    If you are using butter, melt it in a skillet (with an ovenproof handle) over medium heat. (If you are not using butter, just wait until your nonstick skillet has heated up and give it a spritz of your favorite spray oil.) Once the butter has melted, add in the spinach and stir around for a minute or two until it is wilted and reduced in volume somewhat. (This is just to help make sure everything fits in the pan well). Once the spinach has wilted some, toss the chickpeas in, pour the egg mix over it and sprinkle the cheese on top.

  3. 03

    Begin preheating your broiler on high.

  4. 04

    Continue cooking the egg mixture on stovetop at medium to medium low heat until the eggs have begun to thicken up. Time will vary based on how heavy your skillet is and how high your "medium" heat is, but for me it's about 7 minutes. The top will still look wet, but you might notice the eggs starting to come away from the sides of your pan. (Don't worry, you really can't ruin the dish by being off a bit either way, as long as the heat isn't set so high the bottom burns.)

  5. 05

    Finish by moving the skillet under your broiler for just a few minutes for light browning on top. 2 minutes should be enough to get the job done, 5 minutes will give you a browner top, more than that and things start to get tough.

Tips and Notes

  • I always enjoy the little spinach and cheese stuffed triangles of phyllo dough that is real spanikopita. Phyllo dough is fussy to work with, so I asked myself one of my favorite questions: WILL IT FRITTATA? The answer is yes! (Honestly, I'm not sure I can think of a savory pie or pastry that won't frittata though).
  • I've had versions of spanikopita that had no feta and all cheddar, versions that were feta but no cheddar. This has both. Moar cheese can't be wrong.
  • I've added a handful of chickpeas here for some fiber and healthy carbs. You could also add ½ cup of cooked quinoa (one of my favorite additions to frittata) to give it more heft (and about 8g more carbs per serving). This would also go well with some roasted mushrooms (mushrooms and spinach are best friends!), and in the summer, maybe some slow roasted tomatoes.
  • If your doctor or nutritionist has advised you to cut back on dietary cholesterol (mine still does!) or you just want to reduce the calories a little, you can substitute egg whites for part of the eggs (I would say you can get away with substituting up to 3 of the eggs without really having any negative effect on the texture).

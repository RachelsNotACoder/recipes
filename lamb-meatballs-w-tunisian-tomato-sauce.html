Lamb Meatballs with Tunisian Tomato Sauce

Prep / Total 20m / 45m
Tags
Baked Med Lamb
Servings
6

Warmly spiced lamb meatballs, baked hands-off in the oven, then simmered briefly in a cumin-cinnamon tomato and chickpea sauce.

Ingredients

Preparation

  1. 01

    Preheat the oven to 350 degrees.

  2. 02

    Begin the sauce by heating the olive oil in the bottom of a dutch oven or soup pot over medium heat and add the shallots (or onions). Cook, stirring occasionally, until onions are softened, about 5 minutes.

  3. 03

    Add the garlic and spices for the sauce and stir, and cook for about 30 seconds, until spices are fragrant.

  4. 04

    Add the diced tomatoes and chickpeas, cover pot, and lower heat to a simmer.

  5. 05

    Put the meatball ingredients in a large bowl and mix until thoroughly combined.

  6. 06

    Lightly coat 2 baking sheets with spray oil, then form the meat mixture into meatballs about 1 inch in diameter and place on prepared trays.

  7. 07

    Bake in pre-heated oven for about 20 minutes until browned, then add to soup pot with sauce to keep warm for 5-10 minutes while setting the table.

Tips and Notes

  • These meatballs don't include any flour or breadcrumbs. This actually helps them hold up better once they go into the sauce pot. But if you prefer a lighter, somewhat looser ball, you can add ¼ cup of breadcrumbs.
  • These meatballs are browned in the oven instead of on the stovetop. I find that's less messy as well as a lot less effort. To make cleanup even easier, you can put foil down on the sheet pans.
  • This is a hearty meal with generous servings, and the chickpeas in the sauce help make it pretty satisfying. I recommend serving with simply roasted cauliflower (cut up one head of cauliflower, toss flowerets in olive oil, dust with cumin, and roast on a sheet pan at 350 degrees for 30 minutes). Optionally, serve with brown rice or whole wheat couscous.

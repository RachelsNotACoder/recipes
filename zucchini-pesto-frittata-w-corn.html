Zucchini Pesto Frittata w Corn

Prep / Total 10m / 25m
Tags
Skillet US Eggs
Servings
4

Ribbons of zucchini, corn, and melty mozzarella dotted with pesto — a last-hurrah-of-summer frittata.

Ingredients

Preparation

  1. 01

    Crack the eggs into a large bowl with salt and pepper, mix them with a fork or a whisk.

  2. 02

    Heat a skillet (with an ovenproof handle) over medium heat with the butter (if using). Add the mushrooms and cook gently for just 2 minutes or so to give them a head start, then add the zucchini and continue cooking for 3-4 more minutes, to allow the zucchini to just begin to soften.

  3. 03

    Pour in the eggs, being careful to distribute all over the pan. Sprinkle the corn, then the cheese over the top. Dot the top with small dollops of pesto. A little goes a long way!

  4. 04

    Begin preheating your broiler on high.

  5. 05

    Continue cooking the egg mixture on stovetop at medium to medium low heat until the eggs have begun to thicken up. Time will vary based on how heavy your skillet is and how high your "medium" heat is, but for me it's about 7 minutes. The top will still look wet, but you might notice the eggs starting to come away from the sides of your pan. (Don't worry, you really can't ruin the dish by being off a bit either way, as long as the heat isn't set so high the bottom burns.)

  6. 06

    Finish by moving the skillet under your broiler for just a few minutes for light browning on top. 2 minutes should be plenty enough to get the job done, up to 5 minutes will give you a browner top, more than that and things start to get tough.

Tips and Notes

  • This is kind of a last hurrah of summer frittata with the zucchini. Pesto is a good match for the zucchini here, and topped with a little more gooey cheese than usual finishes the whole thing off for a nice hearty combination.
  • If your doctor or nutritionist has advised you to cut back on dietary cholesterol (mine still does!) or you just want to reduce the calories a little, you can substitute egg whites for part of the eggs (I would say you can get away with substituting up to 3 of the eggs without really having any negative effect on the texture).
